Legal Precedent – Framework for Nationwide Title Security

1. Purpose of FSTL Legal Precedent

To establish the legal, constitutional, commercial, and regulatory foundation for the nationwide adoption of Title Insurance, Mortgage Insurance, and Land Security Instruments.

2. Foundations of Nigerian Land Law

  • Land Use Act 1978 – Governor’s Consent & Statutory Right of Occupancy.
  • Property and Conveyancing Law.
  • Supreme Court Judgements on Fraud, Priority & Indefeasible Title.

3. The Gap FSTL Solves

Nigeria has **no unified cadastral certainty**, no nationwide title verification framework, and no insurance protection for buyers, banks, landlords, or investors.

4. FSTL Legal Precedent (Complete)

FSTL is empowered to act as a **private-sector Title Assurance Institution** providing:

  • Title Insurance
  • Mortgage Insurance
  • Title Verification
  • Cadastral Confidence Reports
  • Blockchain-backed Title Logging

5. National Alignment

The system aligns with:

  • Nigerian Insurance Act (as a Special Lines product)
  • CAMA 2020 (corporate authority)
  • Land Use Act (does not restrict indemnity products)
  • Existing global title insurance frameworks

Option B – Policy Government Can Approve Instantly

A national directive allowing FSTL to operate as:

  • Private Title Validation Authority
  • Approved Title Insurance Underwriter
  • Mortgage Insurance Validator for Banks

This allows immediate national rollout without amending the Land Use Act.
