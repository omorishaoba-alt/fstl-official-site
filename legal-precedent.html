Legal Precedent

Authoritative Nigerian property law guidance for all FSTL products.

Key Statutes

  • Land Use Act 1978 – Basis of land ownership and Governor’s Consent.
  • Property & Conveyancing Law – Governs assignments, leases, and conveyancing.
  • Registration of Titles / Land Registration Acts – Controls title perfection and priority rules.
  • Evidence Act & Contract Law – Validity of agreements and admissibility of documents.

Major Case Law Precedents

  • Ogunleye v Oni (1990) – You cannot transfer a better title than you possess.
  • Osho v Foreign Finance Corp (1991) – Registered title takes priority.
  • Savannah Bank v Ajilo (1989) – Governor’s Consent is mandatory.
  • Adeniran v Alao (2002) – Possession cannot override documented title.
  • Idundun v Okumagba (1976) – Five methods of proving title.

Legal Principles Applied

  • Indemnity law for loss compensation.
  • Priority rules for title perfection.
  • Bona fide purchaser doctrine for fraud protection.
  • Asset-certainty principles for securitisation and ABS formation.
