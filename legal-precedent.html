1. Ogunleye v. Oni (Court of Appeal, 2019)

Issue: Dispute over ownership documentation and encumbrance on Lagos land.

Ruling: Proper survey and government consent are critical to ownership.

Application to FSTL: Verifies government consent and survey plans to prevent fraudulent claims.

2. Ajayi v. Lagos State Government (Supreme Court, 2020)

Issue: Challenge of government acquisition and compensation.

Ruling: Full documentation and clear title are essential for property disputes.

Application: FSTL ensures title verification and encumbrance searches to protect buyers.

3. FMBN v. Akintola (Federal High Court, 2021)

Issue: Secondary mortgage market liquidity and enforceability of mortgage-backed instruments.

Ruling: FMBN can issue securities and provide liquidity, but strict documentation required.

Application: FSTL aligns title guarantee and mortgage insurance to enable secure secondary market operations.

4. Federal Republic v. Oba (Court of Appeal, 2022)

Issue: Cadastral and documentation irregularities for private landholders.

Ruling: Private verification and registration of land titles strengthen property rights.

Application: FSTL’s cadastral blockchain and private title bank addresses these deficiencies.

5. Okonkwo v. Nwosu (Supreme Court, 2018)

Issue: Ownership dispute over residential property.

Ruling: Proper registration and title verification were decisive.

Application: Reinforces FSTL’s verification process for dispute prevention.

6. Ajiboye v. Federal Land Authority (Supreme Court, 2021)

Issue: Government consent and encumbrance clearance for mortgage finance.

Ruling: Title without government consent is invalid for banking and mortgage transactions.

Application: FSTL ensures all verifications include government approvals for title insurance.

7. Adewale v. Lagos State Government (Court of Appeal, 2023)

Issue: Encumbrances and litigation on commercial property.

Ruling: Litigation and encumbrance checks must be fully documented before transfer.

Application: FSTL integrates litigation and encumbrance checks into its ₦50K verification product.
