FSTL Legal Foundation

FSTL’s services are built upon a robust understanding of Nigerian statutory law, judicial precedents, and international land-title protection standards. This forms the backbone of our title insurance, mortgage insurance, and land verification products.

Key Nigerian Statutes

  • Land Use Act 1978: Governs land ownership, occupancy rights, and Governor’s Consent.
  • Property and Conveyancing Law: Rules for assignments, leases, and conveyancing documentation.
  • Registration of Titles / Land Registration Acts: Controls title perfection, priority rules, and dispute resolution.
  • Evidence Act & Contract Law: Ensures validity and admissibility of agreements and title documents.

Major Court Cases – Nigerian Land Law

  • Ogunleye v Oni (1990): Demonstrated that one cannot transfer a better title than they legally possess.
  • Osho v Foreign Finance Corp (1991): Established that registered titles take priority over unregistered claims.
  • Savannah Bank v Ajilo (1989): Confirmed that Governor’s Consent is mandatory for valid land transactions.
  • Adeniran v Alao (2002): Held that mere possession cannot override documented title.
  • Idundun v Okumagba (1976): Identified five legally accepted methods for proving land ownership.
