Overview

Municipal and infrastructure bonds can fund urban development, affordable housing, and public utilities without relying on foreign debt.

Benefits

  • Local financing, avoiding foreign currency risk.
  • Encourages municipalities to plan and maintain urban infrastructure.
  • Generates long-term funding for housing projects.
  • Improves investor confidence through secured, regulated instruments.

FSTL Integration

  • Coordinates with state and municipal authorities to issue bonds for housing projects.
  • Links bond funding to verified title and mortgage insurance products.
  • Supports urban land industrialisation to boost economic growth.

Statistics

  • Average municipal bond issuance in developed countries: 15–25% of GDP for infrastructure annually.
  • Nigeria currently relies >80% on federal borrowing; local bond market underutilized.
  • Proper municipal bond framework could generate funding for up to 1 million housing units per year.
