Overview

The Fannie Mae & Ginnie Mae models demonstrate how secondary mortgage institutions can create liquidity and stability in housing finance, providing long-term funding to primary lenders.

Fannie Mae (FNMA)

  • Privately owned, government-sponsored enterprise (GSE).
  • Purchases mortgages from banks, bundles them into mortgage-backed securities (MBS).
  • Enhances liquidity, lowers borrowing costs, and stabilizes the mortgage market.

Ginnie Mae (GNMA)

  • Fully government-backed; guarantees timely payment on MBS.
  • Focuses on low-income and federally insured mortgages.
  • Supports affordable housing without taxpayer risk for conventional loans.

Implications for Nigeria

  • FMBN should emulate Fannie Mae to buy mortgages from banks, freeing capital for new loans.
  • GNMA’s model informs FHA to guarantee low-income housing finance effectively.
  • Integration with title and mortgage insurance reduces lender risk and encourages private bank participation.
