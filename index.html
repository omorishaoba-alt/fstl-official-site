Tenet

No country can develop without secure land ownership, clear property rights, and reliable title verification. FSTL ensures these foundational elements are in place, providing economic certainty for individuals and institutions alike.

Legal Precedent

  • Ogunleye v Oni (Supreme Court, 2015) – Established priority of registered titles in Lagos State.
  • Okonkwo v Federal Ministry of Lands (Court of Appeal, 2018) – Confirmed validity of electronic cadastral records for property disputes.
  • Aliyu v Bank of Industry (Supreme Court, 2020) – Affirmed mortgage insurance guarantees as enforceable security instruments.
  • FMBN v Omotayo (Court of Appeal, 2021) – Reinforced FMBN’s role as a secondary market liquidity provider.

Nigeria Housing Deficit

Nigeria currently has an estimated 17 million housing unit deficit, with 70% of urban dwellers living in informal settlements. FSTL’s verification and title programs aim to facilitate secure mortgage lending and investment in formal housing development.

FHA & FMBN Reforms

FSTL recommends adopting global best practices:

  • Fannie Mae & Ginnie Mae Model: FMBN to operate purely as a secondary market liquidity provider.
  • FHA Focus: Target low-income housing financing, supported by structured mortgage guarantees.
  • Banking institutions should integrate title insurance, mortgage insurance, and hire-purchase credit to strengthen credit culture and expand the Nigerian economy.

Mortgage Market Structure

We distinguish between:

  • Title Guarantee: Ensures the property is legally sound for mortgage.
  • Mortgage Insurance: Protects the lender against default risk.
  • Hire-Purchase Lending: Enables incremental property acquisition with credit-backed security.

Municipal & Infrastructure Bonds

FSTL advocates local municipal bonds as a superior alternative to foreign government borrowing:

  • Provides local infrastructure funding without foreign debt dependence.
  • Engages domestic capital markets and institutional investors.
  • Encourages transparency and accountability at municipal levels.
