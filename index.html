Institutional Infrastructure for Property, Title, and Mortgage Risk in Nigeria.

First Standard Title Limited (FSTL) provides the foundational frameworks required to stabilise property transactions, protect capital, and restore confidence in Nigeria’s real estate market.

Read the White Paper Institutional Engagement

The Market Reality

Nigeria’s property and mortgage markets suffer from structural risk arising from title uncertainty, fragmented registries, and the absence of standardized insurance-backed safeguards.

Title Uncertainty

Overlapping claims and weak verification undermine transactional confidence.

Mortgage Fragility

Lenders face elevated default exposure due to inadequate risk mitigation tools.

Capital Flight

Institutional capital remains sidelined due to regulatory and structural gaps.

Our Role

FSTL operates as market infrastructure — not a brokerage, not a developer, but a stabilising layer that enables trust, compliance, and scale.

Risk Standardisation

Institutional frameworks aligned with global insurance and title standards.

Regulatory Alignment

Designed to complement Nigerian legal and policy structures.

Market Confidence

Creating conditions required for long-term domestic and foreign capital.

NATICON White Paper

The National Title Insurance & Confidence Framework (NATICON) outlines a structured, insurance-backed solution to Nigeria’s systemic property risk.
