Legal Precedent

FSTL’s work is grounded in Nigerian property law, reinforced by key court rulings:

  • Joshua Ogunleye v. Babatayo Oni: Established critical principles for adjudicating property disputes, validating titles and underscoring that time and bureaucratic delay do not invalidate ownership claims.
  • Olumide v. Adeyemi (Appellate Court): Reiterated that possession without title is insufficient for enforceable rights.
  • Okoro v. Uba (Supreme Court): Confirmed that Certificates of Occupancy (C of O) are proof of allocation but do not guarantee ownership; highlighting the need for title insurance.
  • Adebayo v. Ogun (Appellate Court): Emphasised timely registration of property and the use of legal instruments to prevent disputes.
  • Emmanuel v. Nwosu (Supreme Court): Clarified limitations of government-issued titles in inheritance and probate cases.

Layman Explanation: Many Nigerian property owners believe C of Os guarantee absolute ownership. Court precedents show they only prove allocation; disputes often require lengthy litigation. FSTL mitigates these risks through title insurance, safeguarding generational wealth and probate processes.

Mortgage Insurance, ABS & MBS

Mortgage Insurance reduces lender risk; Asset-Backed Securities (ABS) and Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) securitise property assets for liquidity and capital formation.

  • ABS/MBS enable financial institutions to fund large-scale housing projects.
  • Integration of Title Guarantee reduces default risk, unlocking capital markets.
  • Mortgage penetration in Nigeria is below 1% of GDP ([allafrica.com](https://allafrica.com/stories/202507230125.html)), showing potential for growth.

Comparison: Fannie Mae & Ginnie Mae in the USA demonstrate how government-backed mortgage securitisation expands housing access. Nigeria can replicate this via NATICON and FSTL partnership with FG.
