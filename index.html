NIGERIA'S HOUSING DEFICIT

  • Current deficit: **22 million housing units** (Federal Ministry/UN Habitat)
  • Annual demand: **700,000 units**
  • Annual supply: **< 150,000 units**
  • Mortgage penetration: **< 1% of GDP** (G7 = 50–70%)
  • Land documentation delay: **6–24 months** average

This deficit cripples job creation, credit expansion, and national productivity.

WHY NIGERIA STRUGGLES — THE CORE DEFECTS

  • No functioning secondary mortgage market
  • No national land industrialisation strategy
  • Weak cadastral management
  • Fragmented state-level land administration
  • No unified title guarantee system
  • Absence of credit-backed land markets

FHA & FMBN — STRUCTURAL DEFICIENCIES

The Federal Housing Authority and Federal Mortgage Bank were never designed for Nigeria’s modern demographic and urbanisation challenges.

FHA — What Is Wrong

  • Builds units far above low-income affordability
  • Not structured as a volume-based affordable housing developer
  • Insufficient public-private partnerships

FMBN — The Core Problem

  • FMBN should function as a **pure secondary mortgage market institution**
  • It should NOT be issuing mortgages directly
  • It should be buying mortgages from lenders and injecting liquidity

THE FANNIE MAE & GINNIE MAE MODEL

This model built the U.S. mortgage market:

  • Fannie Mae buys mortgages from lenders → frees up capital → expands lending
  • Ginnie Mae guarantees MBS for affordable housing

Nigeria must adopt this model to create liquidity and scale affordable housing.

MUNICIPAL & INFRASTRUCTURE BONDS

Instead of depending on foreign borrowing, Nigeria’s cities can issue bonds to fund:

  • Roads
  • Drainage systems
  • Water & power
  • Industrial zones
  • Mass housing

Municipal bonds deepen domestic capital markets, create jobs, and reduce FX pressure.
