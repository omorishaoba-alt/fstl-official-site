Legal Precedent (Client‑Focused)

Joshua Ogunleye v. Babatayo Oni: Confirms that Certificates of Occupancy alone do not guarantee absolute ownership. Disputes can still arise; proper title verification is essential.

Okoro v. Uba (Supreme Court): C of O is recognized as prima facie evidence but must be validated, reinforcing the need for Title Guarantee.

Other Cases: Highlight the importance of documented, verified title before buying, selling, or using property as collateral.

Title Guarantee & Insurance

Title Guarantee provides enforceable protection of ownership beyond government-issued documents. It secures buyers against fraud, disputes, and latent claims.

For lenders, it makes mortgage issuance safer. For investors, it unlocks the use of property in capital markets.

Mortgage Insurance & ABS/MBS

Mortgage Insurance: Protects lenders if borrowers default, encouraging increased lending.

ABS (Asset-Backed Securities): Converts property cash flows into marketable securities, increasing liquidity.

MBS (Mortgage-Backed Securities): Bundles mortgages, enabling institutional investment.

Land Industrialisation & Economic Growth

Structured land use enables housing, infrastructure, industrial parks, and agricultural estates. Verified titles accelerate private investment and reduce disputes.

Examples:

  • USA: Mortgage market and title systems catalysed suburban expansion.
  • UK: Formal land registry underpins property finance.
  • Germany: Cadastral systems ensure clear titles for industrial development.

PMI, FMBN & FHA Explained

Primary Mortgage Institutions (PMIs): Licensed to provide mortgage loans but often lack liquidity and capital, limiting their reach.

Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN): Should provide liquidity support to PMIs and engage capital markets. Currently underutilised.

Federal Housing Authority (FHA): Intended to build affordable housing but has limited output relative to demand.

Deficiencies: Lack of risk mitigation products, weak integration with capital markets, and slow delivery of housing.

Why Government Should Mandate Title & Mortgage Insurance

Mandatory Title & Mortgage Insurance protects buyers, lenders, and investors. It reduces disputes and unlocks capital for housing and infrastructure.

Benefits for Government:

  • Higher tax compliance from formal property markets
  • Reduced litigation costs
  • Greater foreign and domestic investment
  • Improved urban planning outcomes
