Legal Precedent

Legal precedents reinforce FSTL's rationale. Key cases:

  • Joshua Ogunleye v Babatayo Oni: Establishes that validation of property titles can be adjudicated efficiently if legal frameworks and title guarantees are in place.
  • Olumide v Adeyemi (Appellate Court): Confirms the necessity of documentary proof and highlights risks of relying solely on government-issued certificates.
  • Okoro v Uba (Supreme Court): Reinforces that delays in enforcing land rights result in economic and social loss.
  • Adebayo v Ogun (Appellate Court): Highlights inheritance disputes and validates structured title insurance as mitigation.
  • Emmanuel v Nwosu (Supreme Court): Confirms that time and resources are often wasted in litigating ownership, reinforcing the need for title insurance.

Layman Explanation: Individuals and institutions cannot rely solely on government-issued Certificates of Occupancy. Legal cases show delays, disputes, and costs, making structured FSTL title insurance essential for security, probate, and generational planning.

Title Guarantee & Insurance

Title Guarantee is a legally enforceable confirmation of property ownership, backed by FSTL’s risk management framework.

  • Secures buyers, sellers, lenders, and investors
  • Reduces litigation and probate costs
  • Enables collateralisation for capital market participation
  • Supports generational wealth planning

Mortgage Insurance & Asset-Backed Securities (ABS/MBS)

Mortgage Insurance: Protects lenders against default, allowing more lending at lower risk.

ABS/MBS: Securities backed by property mortgages, creating liquidity for banks and capital markets.

These instruments deepen Nigeria’s capital markets, fund housing, and industrialisation projects.

Governance, Risk & Strategic Insights

Primary Mortgage Institutions (PMIs), Federal Housing Authority (FHA), and Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN) are underperforming:

  • PMIs: limited coverage, weak risk assessment, poor integration with capital markets
  • FHA/FMBN: bureaucracy, slow disbursement, limited market impact

Comparisons:

  • Fannie Mae / Ginnie Mae: Efficient mortgage financing, secondary market liquidity
  • FSTL: Structured products, title insurance, capital market integration to replicate these successes in Nigeria

NATICON: National Title Insurance Company of Nigeria

FSTL proposes a JVC/SPV with FG:

  • Ownership: FSTL 51%, FG/other stakeholders 49%
  • Objective: National title insurance, mortgage insurance, land industrialisation financing
  • Function: Issue title guarantees, manage structured products, enable collateralisation for banks and investors

Rationale: Ensures sustainability, reduces litigation, improves capital market depth, and incentivises formal land markets.
