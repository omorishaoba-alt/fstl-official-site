Executive Summary

FSTL protects land ownership and enables financial growth via collateralisation, mortgage-backed securities (MBS), and title insurance solutions. Over 20 years, we have developed robust verification systems, due-diligence frameworks, and risk management protocols that make property finance reliable and safe.

Legal Precedent

Our services are grounded in Nigerian law and verified legal cases, forming the backbone of secure title verification:

  • Ogunleye v Oni (1990) – Cannot transfer better title than possessed.
  • Osho v Foreign Finance Corp (1991) – Registered title takes priority.
  • Savannah Bank v Ajilo (1989) – Governor’s Consent is mandatory.
  • Adeniran v Alao (2002) – Possession cannot override documented title.
  • Idundun v Okumagba (1976) – Five methods of proving title.

FHA & FMBN Deficiencies

The Federal Housing Authority (FHA) and Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN) have historically suffered from:

  • Delays in housing provision and mortgage approvals
  • Limited collateralisation and verification mechanisms
  • Poor integration with land registry and cadastral systems
  • Inadequate risk management leading to default and fraud

FSTL’s independent systems bypass these inefficiencies, ensuring reliability even without direct government involvement.

Nigeria Housing Deficit

Nigeria faces a housing deficit of approximately 17 million units (World Bank, 2025), driven by:

  • Rapid urbanisation
  • Poor title verification systems
  • Lack of structured mortgage finance
  • Inadequate government-backed housing projects

FSTL addresses these gaps by providing verified title insurance, property due diligence, and mortgage enablement.
