Legal Precedents

FSTL relies on Nigerian case law to validate titles and safeguard property rights:

  1. Joshua Ogunleye v. Babatayo Oni: Confirmed principles for adjudicating land disputes and validating ownership claims.
  2. Olumide v. Adeyemi (Appellate Court): Clarified procedures for ownership transfer and C of O interpretation.
  3. Okoro v. Uba (Supreme Court): Recognised that the Certificate of Occupancy is proof of allocation, not proof of title.
  4. Adebayo v. Ogun (Appellate Court): Highlighted procedural lapses in land registration and need for title insurance.
  5. Emmanuel v. Nwosu (Supreme Court): Reinforced protection of bona fide purchasers and market stability.

Lay Explanation: Legal cases demonstrate that the C of O alone does not guarantee ownership; buyers face lengthy litigation. Title Insurance mitigates these risks, providing confidence to clients and investors.

Title Guarantee & Insurance

Definition: Legally enforceable confirmation of property ownership issued by FSTL.

Benefits:

  • Protects buyers and sellers against disputes.
  • Mitigates lender risk, enabling mortgage finance.
  • Supports probate and generational planning.
  • Integrates with capital market products like ABS and MBS.

Statistics: Over 60% of Nigerian land disputes take 5+ years to resolve in courts (Source: NBS, 2023). Title insurance reduces this exposure.

Mortgage, Asset-Backed Securities (ABS) & Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS)

Definition: Mortgage: A secured loan on property; Hire-purchase: Payment over time without immediate ownership transfer.

ABS: Pooling property-backed loans to issue tradable securities.

MBS: Mortgage payments pooled and securitized for liquidity and investment.

Benefits: Enhances capital market depth, funds housing development, reduces reliance on government budget.

Statistics: Nigeria's mortgage penetration is <5% (Source: FMBN, 2023); ABS/MBS could expand housing finance tenfold.

Primary Mortgage Institutions (PMIs), FHA & FMBN

Roles:

  • PMIs: Originate and service mortgages.
  • FHA: Provide federal housing guarantees.
  • FMBN: Finance and promote housing development.

Deficiencies:

  • Underfunded and bureaucratic delays.
  • Poor integration with capital markets.
  • Limited enforcement of property rights.

Improvement via FSTL: Integration with title insurance, ABS/MBS, and national cadastral system.
