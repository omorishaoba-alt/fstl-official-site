Legal Precedents

Key court cases demonstrate the importance of securing legal title and the limitations of Certificates of Occupancy:

  • Ogunleye v. Oni: Confirms legal principles for adjudicating property disputes; establishes need for documented ownership beyond C of O.
  • Olumide v. Adeyemi: Highlights risks of bureaucratic delays and conflicting claims.
  • Okoro v. Uba: Supreme Court decision reaffirming property rights validation processes.
  • Adebayo v. Ogun: Demonstrates limitations of government-issued documents in proving absolute ownership.
  • Emmanuel v. Nwosu: Reinforces necessity of legal title insurance for risk mitigation.

Explanation for clients: Relying solely on C of O exposes property owners and lenders to disputes, litigation delays, and financial loss. FSTL Title Insurance mitigates these risks and ensures intergenerational wealth transfer.

Title Guarantee & Insurance

Title Insurance provides legally enforceable confirmation of property ownership. It protects against:

  • Defective or fraudulent titles
  • Claims due to prior liens or disputes
  • Bureaucratic delays in the issuance of C of O

Integration: Supports mortgages, asset-backed securities, and intergenerational estate planning. Title Insurance underpins the entire FSTL risk mitigation ecosystem.

Mortgage, ABS & MBS

Mortgage: A loan secured by property, unlike hire-purchase, which is installment-based ownership. Mortgages enable large-scale property development.

ABS & MBS: Asset-backed and mortgage-backed securities securitize property loans to increase liquidity and capital formation.

Impact: Integrating these instruments with FSTL Title Insurance deepens capital markets, attracts investors, and accelerates housing and industrial projects.

PMI, FHA & FMBN

Primary Mortgage Institutions (PMIs) are meant to facilitate lending but suffer inefficiencies in risk coverage.

Federal Housing Authority (FHA) and Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN) provide housing finance but face structural weaknesses:

  • Limited liquidity
  • Bureaucratic delays
  • Inadequate risk management

By contrast, Fannie Mae and Ginnie Mae (USA) provide efficient securitisation, standardisation, and risk mitigation. FSTL proposes replicating these best practices in Nigeria.
