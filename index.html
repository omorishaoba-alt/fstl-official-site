The FSTL Tenet

FSTL was founded on one sacred mission: **to protect property rights and unlock the wealth trapped inside Nigeria’s land market**. For 20 years, FSTL has been the quiet but foundational force behind mortgage security, land verification, and the structural reforms needed to build a modern, functional, wealth-creating real estate market.

FSTL 20-Year Experience

For over two decades, FSTL has developed Nigeria’s first structured mortgage risk framework, refined verification standards, and assisted stakeholders in land due diligence, legal defence, claims resolution, and title clarity operations.

This experience is now being transformed into a national infrastructure via **Title Insurance**, **Mortgage Insurance**, **Blockchain-backed Cadastral Systems**, and **Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS)**.

Mortgage Market Structure

Nigeria’s mortgage market is dysfunctional due to:

  • No title insurance
  • No mortgage insurance
  • No securitisation backbone
  • Weak cadastral systems
  • No municipal-based rating system
FSTL solves this end-to-end.

Title Insurance

Title Insurance protects buyers, lenders, developers, and investors from:

  • Defective titles
  • Double allocation
  • Forgery
  • Unclear survey lines
It is the backbone of a functional real estate market.

Mortgage Insurance

Mortgage Insurance protects lenders from borrower default and enables:

  • Longer repayment periods
  • Lower equity deposits
  • Secondary market expansion
  • Mortgage-backed securities

Nigeria’s Housing Deficit

Nigeria’s housing deficit exceeds **28 million units**, driven by:

  • High construction costs
  • No long-term mortgage financing
  • Land titling inefficiencies
  • Lack of secondary mortgage markets
FSTL provides the framework that solves all four.

Global Mortgage Benchmarks

Successful global housing systems rely on:

  • Title Insurance (USA, Canada)
  • Mortgage Insurance (USA, Australia)
  • Securitisation platforms (Ginnie Mae, Fannie Mae)
  • Reliable national cadastral systems (EU, Korea, Japan)
Nigeria has none — until now.

Fannie Mae Model

The Fannie Mae model standardises underwriting and creates liquidity by:

  • Buying conforming mortgages
  • Packaging them into MBS
  • Providing investor guarantees
This ensures consistent credit access for homeowners.

Ginnie Mae Model

Ginnie Mae guarantees mortgage-backed securities issued by:

  • Federal Housing Administration (FHA)
  • Veterans Affairs (VA)
Its model allows for public-private synergy with zero credit losses.
