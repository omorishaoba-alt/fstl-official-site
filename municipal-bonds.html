Overview

Municipal bonds are debt instruments issued by local governments or municipal authorities to fund infrastructure projects. Unlike foreign borrowing, municipal bonds mobilize domestic capital, reduce foreign exchange risk, and create local investment opportunities.

Why Municipal Bonds Matter for Nigeria

  • Reduces reliance on foreign loans, which often carry high interest rates and conditionalities.
  • Encourages local capital market development and investor participation.
  • Supports sustainable infrastructure financing: roads, bridges, hospitals, schools, and affordable housing.
  • Aligns with FSTL’s mission to industrialize land and strengthen the property sector.

FSTL Role in Municipal Bonds

  • Advises municipalities and local authorities on structuring bond issues.
  • Integrates property verification and title assurance to secure bond-backed projects.
  • Provides investors with risk assessment reports and title-backed guarantees.
  • Ensures transparency, governance, and credibility in bond issuance.

Impact on Economic Development

  • Generates local jobs through infrastructure projects.
  • Mobilizes domestic savings for productive investment.
  • Enhances municipal capacity to plan, develop, and maintain assets.
  • Stimulates the property and housing sector through collateralized municipal bonds.

Statistics & Context

  • Nigeria’s estimated housing deficit exceeds 23 million units (FMBN, 2025).
  • Infrastructure financing gap is over ₦10 trillion annually (World Bank, 2024).
  • Municipal bonds in G7 countries fund over 40% of local infrastructure investments.
