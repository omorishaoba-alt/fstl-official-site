Overview

Nigeria currently relies heavily on foreign borrowing to fund infrastructure. Municipal bonds provide a sustainable alternative by raising capital locally for cities and state projects, reducing foreign exchange risk and interest burden.

Why Municipal Bonds?

  • Local capital mobilization reduces dependency on foreign debt.
  • Interest paid remains in the local economy, stimulating growth.
  • Municipal bonds can finance critical infrastructure, including housing, roads, utilities, and urban development.
  • Improves credit culture and transparency at local government level.

Global Benchmarking

G7 countries and emerging markets have successfully used municipal bonds to finance urban infrastructure, stimulate housing, and reduce sovereign debt reliance. Nigeria can replicate these models with FSTL advisory support.
