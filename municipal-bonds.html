Overview

Nigeria’s traditional government borrowing often relies on external debt, increasing currency risk and long-term liabilities. Municipal bonds can provide a local, sustainable solution to finance infrastructure and urban development.

Why Municipal Bonds?

  • Local funding reduces foreign exchange exposure.
  • Direct financing of municipal infrastructure and urban projects.
  • Encourages private investment in public utilities.
  • Interest returns are retained locally, creating a multiplier effect on the economy.

Global Examples

Countries like the US, UK, and India extensively use municipal bonds to fund local infrastructure. Nigeria can replicate this model to finance roads, utilities, housing, and social amenities without relying on expensive foreign loans.

Integration with FSTL Solutions

Statistics

Nigeria’s urban infrastructure deficit: ~₦40 trillion. Local municipal bond issuance could finance up to 50% of short-term urban projects without increasing foreign debt exposure.
