Overview

The mortgage market in Nigeria is underdeveloped. Low mortgage penetration, inefficient title verification, and limited secondary market support create a bottleneck for credit expansion.

Mortgage Insurance & Title Guarantee

  • Title Guarantee protects lenders and buyers against defective property titles.
  • Mortgage Insurance mitigates default risk, allowing banks to lend more confidently.
  • Integration of ABS (Asset-Backed Securities) enables liquidity creation for primary lenders.
  • Hire-purchase and structured mortgage lending create a reliable credit culture.

Economic Impacts

  • Increased mortgage access for Nigerians.
  • Boost in real estate and construction sector activity.
  • Creation of investable, bankable collateral for banks and investors.
  • Strengthened secondary market for housing finance.

Statistics: Mortgage penetration <5%, annual mortgage issuance <0.5% of GDP. Title defect claims reduce credit confidence by 15–20% annually.

Global Comparison

Nigeria can adopt models like Fannie Mae & Ginnie Mae in the US to separate origination from liquidity creation, allowing FHA and FMBN to focus on their core mandates.
