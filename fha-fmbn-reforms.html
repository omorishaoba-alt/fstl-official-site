FHA & FMBN: Current Landscape

The Federal Housing Authority (FHA) primarily focuses on low-income housing development, while the Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN) serves as a secondary market institution providing liquidity for mortgage lenders. Both institutions are critical but historically underperform due to limited operational focus, inadequate funding, and structural inefficiencies.

Key Deficiencies

  • FMBN operates mainly as a secondary market, lacking primary lending engagement
  • FHA’s scope is often diluted beyond low-income housing, reducing impact
  • Limited mortgage penetration: less than 3% of Nigerians have access to formal mortgage financing
  • Fragmented funding sources, reliance on government allocations and inconsistent foreign borrowing

Proposed Reforms

  • Adopt the Fannie Mae & Ginnie Mae model to create securitised mortgage pools
  • FMBN focuses purely on creating liquidity for banks and mortgage lenders
  • FHA concentrates solely on low-income housing provision, leveraging private developers
  • Encourage commercial banks to support title insurance, mortgage insurance, and hire-purchase lending
  • Introduce municipal bonds to finance housing and infrastructure projects

Strategic Impact

Implementing these reforms will create a robust housing finance ecosystem, expand credit culture, increase mortgage penetration, and stimulate private sector participation in housing development, while reducing reliance on foreign borrowing.
