Overview

The Federal Housing Authority (FHA) and the Federal Mortgage Bank of Nigeria (FMBN) are key players in Nigeria’s housing finance. FSTL advocates targeted reforms to make them more effective and aligned with global best practices.

FMBN: Secondary Market Focus

  • Should operate purely as a secondary market institution.
  • Issue mortgage-backed securities (MBS) to create liquidity for primary mortgage lenders.
  • Support Omo et al (primary lenders) with risk mitigation and scalable capital deployment.

FHA: Low-Income Housing Focus

  • Should focus exclusively on affordable housing delivery.
  • Leverage FSTL title insurance and mortgage insurance to secure low-income mortgages.
  • Partner with banks to expand hire-purchase credit and affordable financing options.

Benefits of Reforms

  • Creates a robust mortgage and housing finance ecosystem.
  • Promotes a credit culture in Nigeria, boosting economic growth.
  • Encourages private investment in housing.
  • Aligns Nigeria’s housing finance with global standards like Fannie Mae and Ginnie Mae.

Statistics

  • Over 90% of Nigerians live in housing units financed outside formal mortgage systems (FMBN, 2025).
  • Secondary mortgage market inefficiencies have constrained Nigeria’s housing finance growth for decades.
  • Reforms can unlock up to ₦2 trillion in mortgage lending annually.
