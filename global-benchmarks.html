Overview

Countries with successful mortgage markets demonstrate robust legal frameworks, secondary markets, and credit-enhancing insurance instruments.

Key Benchmarks

  • United States: Fannie Mae & Ginnie Mae securitization model enabling nationwide mortgage liquidity.
  • United Kingdom: National Mortgage Guarantee programs supporting low-income home ownership.
  • Canada & Australia: Long-term fixed-rate mortgages with government-backed insurance.
  • Singapore: Structured mortgage-backed securities to fund mass housing efficiently.

Lessons for Nigeria

  • Secondary mortgage market institutions like FMBN should focus on liquidity creation.
  • Title and mortgage insurance reduce risk and encourage bank participation.
  • Municipal bonds and infrastructure financing can support housing and urban development.

FSTL Implementation

  • Adapts international best practices to Nigeria’s regulatory and market context.
  • Integrates title verification, insurance, and financing solutions for sustainable housing growth.
