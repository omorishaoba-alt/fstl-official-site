Current Challenges

  • FMBN is a purely secondary market institution with limited origination of mortgages.
  • FHA historically attempts both low-income housing and complex financing roles, leading to inefficiencies.
  • Low mortgage penetration: less than 5% of Nigerians have access to formal mortgage financing.
  • Insufficient title guarantee coverage and lack of risk mitigation increase default rates.

Proposed Reforms

  • FMBN should focus on creating liquidity for banks and mortgage originators (similar to secondary market operations of Fannie Mae).
  • FHA should focus exclusively on low-income housing delivery and risk-sharing mechanisms.
  • Encourage all banking institutions to support mortgage, title guarantee, mortgage insurance, and hire-purchase lending to build a robust credit culture.

Strategic Implications

By realigning FHA & FMBN functions, Nigeria can expand mortgage credit, reduce housing deficit, increase economic activity, and integrate global mortgage best practices.

Statistics: Housing deficit ~17 million units;
Annual mortgage issuance < 0.5% of GDP;
Formal mortgage penetration <5%.
